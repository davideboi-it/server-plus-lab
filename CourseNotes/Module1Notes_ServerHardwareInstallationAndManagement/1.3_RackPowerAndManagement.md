# 1.3 Power Management, Cooling, and Physical Safety

## 1. Power Management and PDUs
**PDUs (Power Distribution Units)** are specialized enterprise power strips designed for rack-mounted equipment. They can be mounted horizontally (1U/2U) or vertically (0U).
* **Basic PDU:** A simple, unmanaged power strip with no monitoring features.
* **Metered PDU:** Displays the electrical load locally to help prevent overloads.
* **Switched PDU:** Allows remote control of individual outlets over the network. This is essential for remotely rebooting frozen servers (Cold Boot).
* **Intelligent/Smart PDU:** Combines metering and switching with logging, alerts, and integration with DCIM (Data Center Infrastructure Management) software.
* **The Golden Rule (Daisy Chaining):** NEVER plug one PDU or power strip into another. This bypasses safe load planning and poses a severe risk of circuit overload and fire.

## 2. Power Calculation and Connectors
Ability to plan power loads safely within a rack.
* **The P-I-E Formula:** To calculate power consumption, use the formula **P = I × E** (Power/Watts = Current/Amps multiplied by Voltage/Volts).
* **The 80% Rule:** The continuous electrical load must NEVER exceed 80% of the maximum rated capacity of the circuit or PDU. *(Example: on a 20A circuit, the safe continuous target load is 16A).*
* **Power Connectors:** * **Twist-Lock Receptacles:** Wall outlets (like NEMA L6-30) often use twist-lock mechanisms to prevent accidental disconnections of critical equipment.
    * **Standard IEC Connectors:** Enterprise servers typically use standard **C13/C14** (standard 10A/15A) or **C19/C20** (heavy-duty 16A/20A) power cables to connect to the rack PDU.

## 3. UPS (Uninterruptible Power Supply) and Redundancy
The primary goal of a UPS is not to keep the server running indefinitely, but to provide a battery bridge long enough to ensure a graceful shutdown or to allow backup generators to start.

### UPS Topologies
* **Offline / Standby UPS:** The cheapest option. The server runs on wall power, and switches to battery only when power fails. There is a slight transfer delay (milliseconds).
* **Line-Interactive UPS:** Includes an automatic voltage regulator (AVR) to handle brownouts and surges without draining the battery, but still has a minor transfer delay.
* **Online Double-Conversion UPS:** The enterprise standard for critical systems (e.g., life-support or core databases). Incoming AC power is continuously converted to DC to charge the battery, and then back to AC to power the server. This guarantees **zero transfer time** and perfectly clean power.

### UPS Best Practices
* **Auto-Shutdown:** Servers must run management software capable of receiving signals from the UPS via network or serial connection to shut down cleanly before the battery depletes.
* **What NOT to connect:** NEVER plug laser printers or heating devices into a UPS. Their massive inrush current can overload the inverter and destroy the unit.
* **A/B Feeds (Redundant Architecture):** Enterprise racks use two completely independent electrical circuits ("A" and "B"), connected to two different UPS units, feeding into the server's dual power supplies. *(Note: A server with two 1100W power supplies operating in redundancy will still draw a maximum total load of 1100W, not 2200W).*

## 4. Rack Cooling and Airflow
Modern rack-mounted devices rely on strict airflow management to prevent thermal shutdowns.
* **Front-to-Back Airflow:** Standard enterprise equipment pulls cold air from the front bezel and exhausts hot air out the rear.
* **Hot Aisle / Cold Aisle:** An architectural layout where racks are arranged in alternating facing rows. This separates the cold intake air from the hot exhaust air, maximizing cooling efficiency and preventing thermal recirculation.
* **Blanking Panels:** Solid metal or plastic panels used to fill empty rack spaces ("U" slots). They are critically required to prevent hot exhaust air from the rear from being sucked back into the cold intake aisle.
* **Switch Airflow:** Network switches can be ordered with "Port Side Intake" or "Port Side Exhaust". Ensure the configuration aligns correctly with the rack's specific Hot/Cold aisle containment strategy.

### Environmental Baselines
* **Optimal Temperature:** 68°F to 75°F (20°C to 24°C).
* **Optimal Humidity:** 45% to 55% Relative Humidity (RH).
    * *Too Dry (< 40%):* Exponentially increases the risk of Electrostatic Discharge (ESD).
    * *Too Humid (> 60%):* Causes condensation and hardware corrosion.

## 5. Physical Safety and Hazard Prevention
* **Weight Balancing:** Always install the heaviest equipment (UPS, massive battery arrays, dense 4U servers) at the very bottom of the rack to maintain a low center of gravity and prevent tipping.
* **Lifting Techniques:** Lift with the legs, never the back. Always utilize mechanical server lifts for heavy 4U nodes or chassis.
* **ESD Safety (Electrostatic Discharge):** Before touching internal server components (like RAM or CPUs), technicians must wear an ESD wrist strap. The alligator clip MUST be attached to an **unpainted, bare metal** surface of the chassis to provide a proper electrical ground.
* **Plenum Cables:** If network cables are run through raised floors or drop ceilings used for HVAC air returns (Plenum spaces), the cables must have a fire-retardant, low-smoke **Plenum-rated jacket** to prevent toxic gas distribution during a fire.
