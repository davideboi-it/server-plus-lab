# Module 3.7 - Physical Security Concepts

## 1. Physical Access Controls

### Physical Security Mechanisms
| Control | Purpose |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Bollards** | Prevent vehicles from ramming or accessing restricted areas. |
| **Fencing** | Creates a physical perimeter to deter unauthorized entry. |
| **Guards** | Provide human surveillance, access control, and rapid response. |
| **Cameras** | Extend guard presence; monitor and record activity for deterrence and review. |
| **Lighting** | Deters unauthorized access and increases visibility for surveillance. |
| **Locks** | Restrict access to authorized personnel using physical keys or codes. |
| **Mantraps** | Control access by isolating individuals in a secured space between two doors. |
| **Safes** | Securely store sensitive equipment, media, or documents against theft. |
| **Shredders** | Destroy sensitive documents or media to prevent unauthorized access. |

### Electronic Locks & Authentication
* **Biometric Lock:** Grants access based on a person's unique physical traits (e.g., fingerprints, iris patterns, facial recognition).
* **Card Reader:** Requires a magnetic stripe or smart card to be swiped, inserted, or tapped to grant access.
* **Radio Frequency Identification (RFID) Lock:** Uses a wireless signal from an RFID tag or badge to unlock a door or gate when within range.

## 2. Architectural Reinforcements
Enhance physical security by making unauthorized access or observation more difficult.

* **Signal Blocking (Faraday Shielding / Tempest):** Prevents wireless signals (e.g., Wi-Fi, Bluetooth) from entering or leaving a secure area to reduce the risk of eavesdropping or signal-based attacks.
* **Reflective or Opaque Glass:** Limits visibility into sensitive areas from the outside, protecting against surveillance or social engineering attempts.
* **Data Center Camouflage:** Conceals the true function of a facility (e.g., using unmarked buildings or false signage) to reduce its likelihood of being targeted.

## 3. Environmental Controls

### Heating, Ventilation, and Cooling (HVAC)
* HVAC supports physical security by maintaining safe temperature and humidity levels.
* Prevents hardware damage, system failure, and potential data loss caused by overheating or condensation.

### Fire Suppression
A fire needs ignition, fuel, oxygen, heat, and a chemical reaction. Disrupting any of these extinguishes the fire. Datacenters use three levels of fire protection:

**1. Building Level Fire Suppression**
* **Tools:** Sprinklers, firewalls, fire-rated floor assemblies, and handheld fire extinguishers.
* **Rule:** There should be a handheld extinguisher for every 3,000 square feet if class A combustible materials (cloth, wood, paper, rubber, plastics) exist in the work area.

**2. Room Level Fire Suppression**
* **Tools:** Sprinklers, water mist, aerosols, or gas (Gas is preferred over water to prevent equipment damage).
* **Aerosol Systems:** Use strategically placed canisters (containing potassium nitrate) that discharge when fire/smoke is detected to disrupt the combustion process.
* **Inert Gas:** (e.g., argon mixed with nitrogen) Displaces oxygen. Requires a large amount of gas to fill the room.
* **Clean Agent Gas:** (e.g., Novec 1230 or FM-200) Absorbs heat and/or displaces oxygen. Requires less gas than inert systems and leaves no residue. *(Note: Halon gas was banned in the 1990s due to ozone depletion).*

**3. Rack Level Fire Suppression**
* **Tools:** Direct-release systems with in-rack fire detection and tubing that delivers the chemical suppressant directly to the affected hardware.

## 4. Environmental Sensor Types

| Sensor Type | Function & Purpose |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Temperature Sensors** | Monitor ambient and rack-level temperatures to prevent overheating and thermal shutdowns. Can trigger alerts or increase cooling. |
| **Humidity Sensors** | Measure air moisture. Typically maintained at **40-60% relative humidity**. Prevents static discharge (if too dry) or condensation/corrosion (if too humid). |
| **Smoke and Fire Detectors** | Detect early signs of fire/overheating and integrate directly with fire suppression systems (e.g., FM-200, inert gas). |
| **Water Leak Detectors** | Placed on the floor or near AC units/pipes to detect leaks and prevent flooding damage. |
| **Power Monitoring Sensors** | Monitor voltage, current, and power quality at outlets or PDUs. Detect surges, drops, or imbalances. |
| **Airflow Sensors** | Detect proper cool air delivery and hot air exhaust to identify airflow obstructions or HVAC failures. |
| **Access Control Sensors** | Detect when doors or cabinets are opened. Integrates with card readers, keypads, or biometric access. |
