# Module 3.6 - Server Decommissioning

## 1. About Server Decommissioning
Server decommissioning is a structured, formal process used to safely retire servers that are no longer needed or supported.
* **Key Benefits:**
    * Reduces security risks by removing unpatched or unmonitored systems.
    * Frees up physical, virtual, and network resources.
    * Prevents data leaks and avoids accidental service disruptions.
    * Maintains accurate IT asset records and ensures compliance with data/asset policies.
* **Governance:** The process must always be governed by company policies and coordinated across IT, security, and business units.

---

## 2. Server Decommissioning Steps
When retiring a server, a strict sequence of steps must be followed to ensure no data is lost and no active services are unexpectedly disrupted.

1. **Follow Company Policies:** Always adhere to established organizational guidelines first.
2. **Confirm Status:** Verify with stakeholders that the server and its hosted services are truly no longer needed.
3. **Document the Retirement:** Record critical details including:
    * Hostname, IP address, and OS version.
    * Decommission date and the reason for retirement.
    * System owner and contact info.
    * Replacement details or workload migration paths (if applicable).
4. **Update Records:** Update all network diagrams, asset inventories, and service catalogs to reflect the changes.
5. **Remove from Inventory:** Officially remove the server from the active IT asset inventory system.
6. **Submit a Change Request:** Formally request the decommissioning through the organization's change management process.
7. **Archive Data:** Archive system logs, configurations, or business data required for legal or compliance reasons before shutting down.

---

## 3. Data Destruction and Hardware Disposal
Once the server is completely offline and data is archived, the physical hardware and storage must be handled securely.

* **Media Sanitization:** Wipe or physically destroy all storage media to prevent data leakage. Accepted physical destruction methods include:
    * **Degaussing:** Using a powerful magnet to wipe magnetic media (e.g., HDDs, tapes).
    * **Shredding / Crushing:** Physically breaking the drives into small pieces.
    * **Incinerating:** Burning the media to ash.
* **Hardware Disposal:** Repurpose, recycle, or dispose of the remaining physical hardware (chassis, RAM, CPUs) according to company policy and local environmental regulations (e-waste laws).
